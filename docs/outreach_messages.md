# Outreach Messages

Cold outreach works when it's short, specific, and asks for something small. Nobody owes
you a job; people *will* give you 15 minutes if you're clearly serious and make it easy.

Rules for all of these:
- **Personalize the first line** — reference something real (their post, their talk, a
  project). Generic = ignored.
- **Lead with the finding, not your resume.** The coffee-filter insight is your foot in
  the door; it proves you can do the work.
- **Ask for one small thing** (15 min, or feedback), never "a job."
- **One link, max.** Two links reads as spam.

---

## 1. To an FDE at a target company (peer-to-peer, coffee chat)

> Hi [Name] — I saw you're an FDE at [Company] and have been following the role closely as
> I move into it. I just built a small end-to-end project to learn the loop: streamed a
> real Amazon reviews dataset, had an LLM extract structured signal per review, and shipped
> a weekly "what's going wrong on which product" report. It surfaced a coffee filter with a
> fine 3.8-star average that was quietly failing — 4 of its 9 negative reviews independently
> said the coffee came out weak.
>
> Would you be open to a 15-minute chat about what the FDE job is *actually* like day to
> day at [Company]? Happy to work around your schedule. Repo here if useful: [REPO URL]

## 2. To a hiring manager / recruiter (there's an open role)

> Hi [Name] — I'm applying for the [role] on your team and wanted to reach out directly.
> Rather than just send a resume, I built a project that mirrors the FDE loop: take messy
> real-world data and ship something that finds a problem worth money. On a real Amazon
> reviews dataset it flagged a product whose 3.8-star average hid a clear defect pattern,
> and turned it into a recommended action for the (imagined) customer.
>
> 3-min demo: [DEMO URL]. I'd love 15 minutes to walk through how I'd approach a real
> engagement. Is there a good time this week or next?

## 3. LinkedIn connection request note (≤ 300 characters)

> Hi [Name] — moving into Forward Deployed Engineering and just shipped a project on the
> FDE loop (real dataset → LLM extraction → an insight a customer can act on). Would love
> to connect and, if you're open to it, hear what the role's really like at [Company].

## 4. Warm follow-up (after they viewed your profile / liked the post)

> Thanks for taking a look, [Name]! Since [Company]'s team does exactly this kind of
> customer-facing build work, I'd really value 15 minutes of your read on my project and
> where I'd need to level up. No pressure if the timing's bad — either way, appreciate it.

---

## Sequencing (how to actually run this)
1. **Post the LinkedIn write-up first.** Warm outreach beats cold — people who've seen your
   post are far likelier to reply.
2. **Then send 5–10 personalized messages a week.** Quality over volume; each one references
   something specific about the person.
3. **Peers before managers.** An FDE who likes your project can refer you internally — worth
   10x a cold application.
4. **Track it.** A simple sheet: name, company, date, response. Follow up once after ~5
   business days, then let it go.
5. **Always ask for one referral or one name at the end of a good chat** — "Is there anyone
   else you'd suggest I talk to?" That's how the network compounds.

## What NOT to do
- Don't paste the same message to 50 people — it shows, and it burns your name.
- Don't lead with "I'm looking for a job." Lead with the work.
- Don't attach your resume in a cold DM. Earn the conversation first.
- Don't over-apologize or hedge ("sorry to bother you…"). Be brief and confident.
